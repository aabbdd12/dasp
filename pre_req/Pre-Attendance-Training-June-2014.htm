Pre-attendance preparation for the course:

Microeconomic Analysis of Welfare and Policy - ECN U012

16-20 June 2014

 

The equivalent of one-day distance preparation and homework is required on the part of all participants before attending the 16-20 June course at Université Laval in Canada. Participants will learn to use two distributive analysis packages (DAD and DASP) in addition to getting to know the Stata software (for those with no previous knowledge of it). Distance facilitators will be available to assist during that pre-attendance period.

You are invited to send your pre-attendance preparation for the course ECN-U012 homework by email (pep-school@pep-net.org)

If you encounter any technical problems, please do not hesitate to contact us, and we will be happy to provide the necessary support.

The Stata software

The Stata software has become in the last 20 years a popular tool for processing data. It comes with a large number of basic data management modules that are efficient for working with large datasets. The flexibility of Stata also enables programmers to provide specialized programming routines to add to the power of the software.

Participants need to be able to use Stata before and during their course at Laval. For those who do not have Stata installed on their laptop, please write to the PEP-Laval School for alternative arrangements.

DAD: Distributive Analysis // Analyse Distributive

DAD is designed to facilitate the analysis and the comparisons of social welfare, inequality, poverty and equity across distributions of living standards. It is available for free.
