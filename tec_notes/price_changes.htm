Note: This paper shows how to conduct distributive analysis after the estimation of changes in prices, wages and interest rates in a CGE model following some economic shocks.


Duclos, Jean-Yves, Paul Makdissi and Quentin Wodon (2003). "Poverty-Reducing Tax Reforms with Heterogeneous Agents," Cahier de recherche 0313, CIRPEE.

Note: The poverty impact of indirect tax reforms is analyzed using sequential stochastic dominance methods. The theoretical framework of this study may be applied to changes in prices following some shocks or changes in indirect taxation.


Duclos, Jean-Yves, Paul Makdissi and Abdelkrim Araar (2009). "Pro-Poor Tax Reforms, with an Application to Mexico," IZA Discussion Paper 4511, Institute for the Study of Labor (IZA).

Note: This paper proposes a methodology for testing for whether tax reforms are pro-poor. This is done by extending stochastic dominance techniques to help identify tax reforms that will necessarily be deemed absolutely or relatively pro-poor by a wide spectrum of poverty analysts.

 

Sami Bibi & John Cockburn & Luca Tiberti & Massa Coulibaly, 2009. "The impact of the increase in food prices on child poverty and the policy response in Mali," Innocenti Working Paper inwopa09/66, UNICEF Innocenti Research Centre.

 

Note: This study presents simulations of the impacts of the recent food crisis and a number of policy responses with respect to the welfare of children. The impacts are analyzed in terms of monetary (food) poverty, nutrition, education, child labor and access to health services of children
